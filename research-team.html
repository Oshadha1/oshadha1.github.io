Prof. Lilantha Samaranayake is head of the department in Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, the University of Peradeniya since 2007. He has been a visiting researcher at the Graduate School of Life Science and Systems Engineering, Kyushu Institute of Technology, Kitakyushu, Japan. From 2009 to 2011 he was a Senior Scientist in National Nanotechnology Initiative under the Ministry of Technology and Research. He was the Section Chair of the Institute of Electrical & Electronic Engineers (IEEE) Sri Lanka Section for the year 2010. Further, he was a member of the Board of Governors of the Arthur C. Clerk Institute for Modern Technologies, Moratuwa, Sri Lanka from 2010 to 2013. In addition, he has been a member of the National Committee on Electronics and ICT and a member of the Research Panel on Engineering Science and Architecture of the National Science Foundation for the same period. He has been a Research Fellow in Control of Electric Machines in the Centre for Automotive Engineering in the School of Aerospace, Transport, and Manufacturing Engineering, at the Cranfield University, United Kingdom 2014/2015, where he worked on the development of FUTURE (Fundamental Understanding of Technology of Ultra Reduced Emission) vehicles funded by UK's EPSRC. His research includes applications of nanotechnology in polymer photovoltaics, electric powertrains, and control, DC grids, and control systems in general. He is a Senior Member of the IEEE

Prof. Manjula Fernando received his BSc(Eng) degree with first-class honors from the University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka in 1993 and he was awarded the inaugural Ceylon Electricity Board Gold Medal and Prize for the best performance in Electrical and Electronic Engineering. He received his Technical Licentiate degree from the Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden, and his Ph.D. degree from Chalmers University of Technology, Sweden in 1997 and 1999 respectively. In May 1993, he started his career as an instructor in the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, University of Peradeniya, and at present, he is working as a Professor. During his career, he was a guest researcher at Fachbereich Elektrotechnik, HTWS, Zitau GERMANY during June/July 1997, a guest researcher at Chalmers University of Technology SWEDEN during July/August 2000, and an academic fellow at the Manchester University UK during September 2006 to February 2007. He is a senior member of IEEE. He was the founding chair of the IEEE Sri Lanka section Power and Energy Society chapter in 2010, the chair of the IEEE Sri Lanka Central Region subsection in 2009, and the general chair of the Fourth IEEE International Conference on Industrial and Information Systems (ICIIS2009) in 2009. He is a Charted Electrical Engineer, International Professional Engineer, and member of the Institution of Engineers (Sri Lanka).
